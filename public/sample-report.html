🔍Top Major Deep-Dive — Computer Science

Computer Science isn't just about coding — it's about learning to think systematically about complex problems. Your student's profile maps directly onto what CS rewards: analytical thinking, comfort with abstraction, and the drive to build things that work.

The day-to-day in a CS degree involves a mix of theoretical work (algorithms, data structures, computational theory) and applied projects (building software, working in teams, solving real problems). The first two years are foundational and can feel abstract — but the upper-level courses are where most students find their specific passion, whether that's AI, security, systems, or human-computer interaction.

What makes CS different from coding bootcamps: A CS degree teaches you to think about problems from first principles. You'll understand why certain algorithms are faster, how operating systems work under the hood, and how to design systems that scale. That depth is what separates CS graduates from self-taught developers in technical interviews at top companies.

Emerging path to watch: AI/ML Engineering. This didn't exist as a mainstream career 10 years ago. Today it's one of the highest-paid and fastest-growing roles in tech. A CS degree with a focus on machine learning courses is the most direct path.
